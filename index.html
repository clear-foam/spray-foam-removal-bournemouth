Understanding the Issue

What Is Spray Foam Removal Bournemouth and Why Is It Such a Big Problem for Dorset Homeowners?

Spray foam insulation also known as spray polyurethane foam (SPF) was heavily marketed to UK homeowners throughout the 1990s, 2000s and early 2010s as an energy-saving solution. Thousands of properties across Bournemouth, Poole, Christchurch and wider Dorset had it applied to their roof rafters and loft spaces.

The problem? When applied incorrectly or when the wrong foam type is used spray foam can trap moisture against your roof timbers, restrict the natural ventilation your roof structure needs, and lead to rot, mould and structural decay that is hidden from view until serious damage has already occurred.

Today, spray foam insulation is considered one of the most significant hidden defects in the UK housing market. Spray foam removal has become essential not optional for homeowners across Bournemouth, Poole, Dorset, Hampshire and Wiltshire who want to sell, remortgage, or release equity from their property.

Two Types of Spray Foam Both Can Cause Problems

  • Closed-cell spray foam the denser, more rigid type. Forms a near-impenetrable barrier that completely blocks roof ventilation and makes it impossible for surveyors to inspect your roof timbers. Highest risk to mortgage applications.
  • Open-cell spray foam softer and more flexible. While slightly more breathable, it still traps moisture and is flagged by virtually all UK mortgage lenders. Removal is generally less complex but still essential before selling or remortgaging.

The Real Risks of Spray Foam Insulation for Bournemouth & Dorset Properties

Many homeowners across Poole, Christchurch and wider Dorset have discovered the hard way that spray foam insulation installed decades ago is now preventing mortgage approvals, hiding structural damage, and reducing property values.

Mortgage Refusals & Property Devaluation

The single biggest issue. Over 72% of UK mortgage lenders will decline or restrict lending on properties with spray foam insulation in the roof space, regardless of how well it was installed. This directly affects your ability to sell your Dorset property or remortgage it.

💧

Trapped Moisture & Timber Decay

Spray foam seals the roof space from natural ventilation. Condensation builds up against your wooden roof trusses and rafters, causing rot that can go undetected for years. By the time it's visible, structural repairs can cost tens of thousands of pounds.

🔍

Blocked Structural Inspections

RICS surveyors cannot assess the condition of your roof timbers through closed-cell foam. This means buyers, lenders and insurers cannot verify the structural integrity of your roof making your property effectively unsellable without removal.

🌿

Mould, Air Quality & Health Risks

Trapped moisture creates ideal conditions for mould growth. Deteriorating foam can also off-gas volatile organic compounds (VOCs). For Bournemouth and Dorset homeowners with children or respiratory conditions, this is a direct health concern.

In most cases, spray foam removal itself is not covered by standard home insurance policies, as it is considered a pre-existing condition or a remediation of a known defect rather than accidental damage. We recommend speaking to your insurer directly, and we can provide a written condition report to support any claim.
